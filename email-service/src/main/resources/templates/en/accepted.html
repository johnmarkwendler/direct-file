What's next?

If you're owed a refund, you should get it within 21 days by the payment method you chose (either direct deposit or mail); however, sometimes a refund can take longer than 21 days. You can check your refund status 24 hours after the IRS accepts your tax return.

If you owe a federal tax balance and scheduled your payment by electronic funds transfer, the transfer will occur on the scheduled date. Generally, if you owe a federal tax balance, you'll need to pay by the federal tax filing deadline (April 15) using one of these payment options. Your deadline may be postponed if you were affected by a disaster situation.

If it's past the federal tax filing deadline and you didn't schedule a payment, make your payment as soon as possible to limit any interest and penalties.

Don't forget about state taxes and credits

Depending on your situation, you may have to file a state tax return in addition to a federal tax return. You may also be eligible for state tax credits.

If you need to file a state return, sign in to Direct File to be guided to your state's free tax filing tool.
